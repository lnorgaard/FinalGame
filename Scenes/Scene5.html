Room 5

Yet, something makes you uneasy. Maybe it’s the illness, but a feeling in your gut makes you want to turn away. You begin to back up, but then again where can you go? Back to your room to die? Noticing your hesitance, the person pauses their approach, hands raising in innocence. “Hey, hey, don’t be scared...” They speak softly. “I know, it’s a lot, and you look like you haven’t gotten your meds in a while. But I can help you! We can get out of this together.”

“Get out of what? Who are you?”

The person looks surprised hearing your voice for the first time, full of defensiveness. “I’m Alex. And this hospital. We’ll maybe not that quite yet, but at least this floor, and then hopefully to a cure.”
